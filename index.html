Dragonwake

Dragonwake is an ASCII roguelike set in Valoria, a battered frontier built on the corpse of the old empire of Istaris. You come ashore after a storm with a dormant blackglass star amulet tied to a forgotten seal-priest bloodline, just as dragons, blackglass, and old ruins begin waking again.

Expect dangerous roads, town hubs, dungeon descents, readable old-school combat, and a campaign that starts personal and grows into a fight over what should be done with the power under the Sundering Scar.

Quick Play Guide

  • Every action is a turn. Move one step, attack, rest, or interact, then nearby enemies act.
  • Press e to interact. Talk to people, open doors, and use exits with the same key.
  • Default movement is classic roguelike. Use arrows for cardinal movement and Home, End, PgUp, and PgDn for diagonals.
  • Laptop note: on compact keyboards those diagonal keys are usually on an Fn layer. If movement feels awkward, keep one hand on arrows and use the function layer for diagonals.
  • Useful keys: space or . wait, t ranged attack, shift+r rest, 3 inventory, 4 equipment, 6 quest log, 7 skills, 8 spells, z class actions, esc menu or back.
  • Early route: speak to people at Lowwater Docks, then head inland toward Havenford for the first real town and quest hub.
  • If you are new to roguelikes: play slow, inspect things, retreat from bad fights, and treat survival as progress.

Game Details

  • Hand-built campaign structure: storm-wrecked docks, strained frontier towns, mountain holds, blackglass breaches, and deeper dungeon descents under the Scar.
  • Readable ASCII presentation: fast-loading browser build, keyboard-first play, and a direct turn-based combat loop without visual clutter.
  • Classic class chassis: the current playable set centers on Fighter, Ranger, Paladin, Rogue, Cleric, and Wizard.
  • Old-school adventure pressure: traps, hunger, ranged combat, spells, shops, corpse risks, gear upgrades, and hard fights that punish rushing.
  • Setting and story: ruined empires, knightly orders, refugee roads, blackglass relics, and the slow return of dragons rather than a clean heroic fantasy reset.
  • Player hook: your blackglass star amulet and bloodline make you central to powers that most of the world only half remembers.
